Historical Traffic: The Decreasing Retention Rate

Historical traffic data from the COPA database retrieved in Sep. 2019.

In the database, the traffic-related data was not recorded directly, so we used users' content view history to calculate the page views, page views per user, and unique visitors by counting the number of records. The number would be different from the results provided by Google Analytics but are still good for reference.

Since 2014, the daily page views is about 20,855 on average. There are, every day, about 759 unique members would visit the website. For each member, he or she would like to read about 27 content (forum posts, wiki posts, and blog posts). With the area charts under these numbers, we can see that, between 2017 and 2018, there was an abnormal peak for the page views, while there were not many new members visited the website. Per the trending, we can find that there are more and more members visited the website daily, but the increase is pretty small. Also, the number of content the members read consists for a long time. According to these, considering the industry context, COPA website seems performed well.

Retention Rate (One-Year)

However, the retention rate seems to tell us a different story. The two stair-like charts on the left are the cohort analysis for each year. Every column represents the all COPA members who joined COPA within that year, a.k.a. cohort. Each row shows how many members or what percent of that cohort members stop visiting or doing anything on the COPA website during that year (the last activity record). To keep simple, we can only check the numbers on the diagonal. Due to the website migration, all members joined COPA before 2008 are recorded as the 2008 cohort, so we can begin with 2009 cohort members to eliminate the impact. We can see that more and more members stop visiting the website in the first year they joined. The line chart on the right also shows the percentage of each cohort's members who retained or continued to have activity records after one year. The one-year retention rate decreased from 68% to 21%. It seems that the website cannot retain users.

The potential reason could be:

  • The decline of the Cirrus aircraft.
  • Enternal environment changed (e.g. economy)
  • Website/Forum like COPA is not the popular social medium any more.
  • The functions of the website are not convenient for users.

    • With the increasing the slowly increasing visitors, our analysis would focus on the last one: if there are some inconvenient functions on the website.
