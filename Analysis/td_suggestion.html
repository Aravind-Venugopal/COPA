Suggestion Posts Analysis

Analyzing posts of the website suggestion and issue forum from the COPA database retrieved in Sep. 2019.

On the previous COPA website, there is a forum called 'Website Issues & Suggestions', where COPA members can report some issues they met or ask for help with the website problem. Conducting an analysis on the posts in this forum section could provide insights on what the members complained about. The scatter chart below has a date range filter. By moving the two ends, you can select the posts within that date range.

The 2013-2014 Migration: As the findings in historical traffic and forum page, if we move the date range filter to 2013 Q4 - 2014 Q1, we can see that the number of posts in this forum section increased radically, and the content of those posts contained the system slow down issues and complaints about COPA 3.0. There were also many posts asking 'how-to' questions and some website bugs. Recently, COPA completed its fourth migration to the Discourse platform. We can also see some posts about 'how-to' questions and complaints about the change. From there we can find that for COPA's migration, providing enough tutorial information to help with the new website adaption is very important.

The sentiment and subjectivity bar charts on the right provide a reference about whether the posts are very negative (sentiment score below 0, people are very angry or sad) and whether the expression is objective (subjectivity score less than 0.5, without very emotional words). The method to get these scores is using an NLP Python library, called TextBlob, to process the text. Although judging the sentiment and subjectivity of a text is arbitrary, these scores can still provide a relevant reference. By click the bar, you can highlight the post title in the left chart.

We also summarized the most frequent bigrams in this forum section. The bigrams can reflect the entity that people complain about. We can see that the search_result is the top 4 entity mentioned in this forum section. This is also why we want to build up a search result re-ranking model to help COPA members find the most relevant information.
