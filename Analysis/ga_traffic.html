Website Traffic Overview

Overview of website traffic, traffic sources, and the industry benchmark (Club&Organization) of the COPA website since April 2nd to April 18th.

The COPA website migrated to the new system on April 2nd. On the migration day, there were 1,674 uses visited the new website. On the next day, the number of users increased to 3,103, about 85.36% higher than the previous day. However, after that, the number of users decreased to about 2,000 users. On April 11th, there were only 1,425 users visited the website.Although the number of users decreased, the average session duration increased since April 3rd, indicating that users spent more time on the website.

As for the page views per session, on April 2nd and 3rd, the page views per session was low but increased later, demonstrating that users did not read many pages/posts at the beginning but became more and more comfortable about digesting pages on the new website. The bounce rate also decreased from 34.78% to 16.07% since the migration, which supports the conclusion.

Based on data from April 2nd to 19th, users, on average, generated about 4 sessions on the website and viewed about 10.54 pages per session. For each session, users spent about 24 minutes, and 19.59% of users visited the website and quit immediately without doing anything.

User Retention

This is the cohort analysis and shows the retention rate of each cohort. Users who visited the website on April 2nd tend to have a better retention rate in the following 12 days. To be noticed, Google Analytics does not recognize users by their membership ID but the browser cookies. Thus, the new-user group may contain returning users if they change the network or use another device. However, such a situation would not impact the data a lot. We can still conclude that many COPA members visited the new website at the date of migration, and many of them are very active members who often use the COPA website since the retention rates of that cohort are high. Users who visited the new website after the first 3 days are not very active and the retention rates of those cohorts are very low. COPA can continue to track the performance of different cohorts (by day, week, month)ï¼Œand take different actions to see the impact (e.g. retention, pageviews, and session duration) on these cohorts.

Industry Benchmark (Club & Organization)

Google Analytics provides an insightful tool - Benchmarking - for people to compare the website performance to the industry average. By choosing a similar industry from 1600 categories and setting the location and the size, COPA can have more insightful data. When setting the benchmark as the clubs and organization, COPA's website performed pretty well except COPA does not attract many users. It makes sense due to the high entry barry of owning an aircraft. To be noticed, the new users would decrease further while more and more existed members visit the website and become returning users.
