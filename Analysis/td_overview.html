Overview: Analysis on Archieved Data

Analysis of historical data from the COPA database retrieved in Sep. 2019.

Background Information: Working on COPA Initiative project, we got COPA website's database during Dec. 2019. The database was retrieved in Sep. 2019 and contained all data the website have, including text data of all forum, blog, and wiki posts. The database was used for the data analysis and model building. With the database exploration and data cleaning, we analyzed COPA website data and created a Tableau dashboard [Download] in 5 dimensions:

  • Historical Traffic: Pageview, the number of visitors, and the retention rate.
  • Engagement: View vs. reply, engagement time, question posts, and reference.
  • Forum, Blog, Wiki: forum section(category) and posts, blog posts, and wiki posts.
  • Members: New member vs. existed member and member characteristics.
  • Suggestion Posts Analysis: What people suggested or complaint about.

    • Analysis Purpose: This analysis and dashboard aim to provide COPA members, website administrators, and management team a comprehensive understanding of the website performance in the past. The analysis summarized the data in a high-level and hope to bring some useful findings that can help with the future improvement.

    Findings & Recommendations:

  • According to the decreasing retention rate, COPA is hard to retain members now. Learning why COPA members left and take some actions to retain COPA members are important for the organization.
  • Based on the analysis of feedback from COPA members, there are a lot of complaints and website issue report during the COPA 3.0 migration. Better and reliable website service, enough and helpful new website tutorials, and in-time technical and emotional support are the keys for successful migration.
  • Other complaints include email related issues, membership subscription guide, and search engine results. COPA can use our analysis methods and dashboard for keeping monitoring members' needs and try to satisfy them.
  • While the retention rate decreasing, there are not many new COPA members join in the organization. The existing members are the major group who visited the website.
  • With the analysis of blog and wiki posts, the use of blog and wiki decreased significantly, and there are not many COPA members read or contribute to them anymore. Considering the high-quality information provided by blog and wiki, we suggest that COPA can implement our Killer APP to help COPA members search for useful information which comes from both internal and external sources.
  • There were too many forum sections (categories) in COPA 3.0. Some of them, such as Cirrus Flying, GA forum, and Guest Discussion, were busy in the past but cool down in recent years. Some forum sections, like Marketplace and Airframe&Powerplant, were like take away the traffic from old popular forum sections. Reducing the number of forum sections and management the purpose of each forum section can provide COPA members with a user-friendly experience to explore the forum.
  • We recommend that COPA can continue conducting such ad-hoc data analysis on the website performance data and text data that contain COPA members' suggestions. Our analysis can provide a reference for future studies.

    • Limitations: Since we created the dashboard with the local archived database, the analysis can not reflect the website's performance in realtime. However, because the new website is holding on AWS, COPA administrators have the opportunity to connect the Tableau dashboard with the database on the cloud.
